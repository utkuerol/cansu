Biography

Atiye (pronounced as “attyeay”) is a PhD candidate in the Operations, Information and Decisions Department at The Wharton School. She uses empirical methods to investigate the effect information technologies have on our lives. Her research interests can be gathered around two main streams. First, she is interested in understanding the role of information technology (IT) in health care. More specifically, she analyzes both economic and societal effects of these technologies on insurers, care providers and patients, and how IT reshapes the provision of health care services and the connections between providers. She is particularly inspired by the potential benefits of IT for improving care provided to vulnerable populations. In her previous work, she analyzed the benefits of Electronic Medical Records (EMR) adoption by long-term care providers. In the second stream she focuses on data privacy, aiming to understand the value of data and other factors that determine this value.

Atiye earned her bachelor’s degree in Industrial Engineering from Koç University in Istanbul, Turkey. Prior to joining Wharton, she worked as the Strategy and Business Development Consultant of Siemens Healthineers’ Turkey region and as a management consultant at EY (Ernst & Young), where she took part in large scale ERP (Enterprise Resource Planning) transformation projects.
